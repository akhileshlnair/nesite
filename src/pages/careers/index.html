Machine Learning Engineer

Research • Gainesville, FL / Remote • Equity Only

The Role

We are looking for engineers obsessed with efficiency. At Nair Engineering, we aren't training massive models on cloud clusters. We are taking state-of-the-art medical intelligence and compressing it into a consumer device.

You will work on the bleeding edge of quantization, sparsity, and speculative decoding. Your code will run on the NPU, GPU, and CPU of mobile devices, delivering life-saving insights with 0ms latency and 100% privacy.

About You

You don't need a PhD (though we like them). You need to have strong experience with building your own langauge models and a willingness to learn our own Swift/Metal based workflow. It is preferred that you understand healthcare workflows. Patient care is the point of this project, our language models will be ineffective without an understanding of this.

  • Strong experience with Python, Pytorch, and Swift.
  • Good understanding of the mathematics and worksflows of LLMs and other machine learning models (the more the better).
  • High GPA: A GPA isn't a measure of intelligence, but rather a measure of discipline, hard work, and committment. If you can't show up to a simple class, you likely won't show up to meetings and you'll waste our time. Exceptions will be made, of course.
  • Fast Learner: if you can't learn fast you'll likely fall behind. Learning fast isn't about becoming a genius in the topic, it's about knowing enough to move forward. Move fast, minimize mistakes, and ship.

iOS Engineer (Metal)

Engineering • Gainesville, FL / Remote • Equity Only

The Role

This isn't building a weather app in SwiftUI. This is building experimental frontier models that save lives. Swift/Metal has little documentation when it comes to machine learning, you will need to learn fast. You will need to decode raw Pytorch code and learn to translate it into Swift/Metal. This is hard. We don't expect to even fill this role.

The Stack

  • Deep Swift: Decoding messy Pytorch, building new engines, experimenting to the max.
  • Metal: Writing custom shaders for compute tasks.

Requirements

  • You have built custom metal shaders: can be for video games, ML, or other novel applications.
  • You have strong problem solving skills. Math at UF should be a joke and solving puzzles should be fun for you.
  • You care about efficiency: many people depend on Vivral, we need our models to be faster than fast, quicker than quick.

Machine Learning Engineer

Engineering • Remote / NYC • Equity Only

The Role

We are looking for engineers who are obsessed with efficiency. At Nair Engineering, we are not training massive models on cloud clusters. We are taking state-of-the-art medical intelligence and compressing it into the secure enclave of a consumer device.

You will work on the bleeding edge of quantization, sparsity, and speculative decoding. Your code will run on the NPU, GPU, and CPU of mobile devices, delivering life-saving insights with 0ms latency and 100% privacy.

About You

You don't need a PhD (though we like them). You need to understand how computers actually work. We prefer students, dropouts, and hackers who have contributed to open-source projects like `llama.cpp` or `tinygrad` over those with corporate accolades.

  • Strong experience with Python and C++.
  • Deep understanding of Transformer architecture internals (KV-cache management, attention mechanisms).
  • Experience with model compression techniques (Quantization, Pruning, Distillation).
  • Familiarity with on-device inference frameworks (CoreML, ONNX Runtime, TFLite).
  • Ability to read a paper on Arxiv and implement it over the weekend.

The Tech Stack

  • Languages: Python, C++, Swift, Metal Shading Language.
  • Frameworks: PyTorch, MLX (Apple Silicon), CoreML.
  • Hardware: Apple Neural Engine, Mobile GPUs.

The Deal

This is not a 9-to-5. This is a mission. We are a small, tight-knit team. We do not pay salaries. We pay in generous stock options. If we succeed, you own a piece of the future of medicine. If we fail, you learned how to build the impossible.
